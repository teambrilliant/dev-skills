# PR-FAQ — the template

The Working Backwards artifact: a mock **press release** (the customer-facing
end-state) + an **FAQ** (the hard internal/external questions). Written when an
idea is formed; iterated 10+ drafts before any build. Length is a forcing
function — keep it tight.

## Part 1 — Press Release (< 1 page)

Write it as if the product already launched. Customer benefit first, technology
last. Plain language a customer would understand — no jargon.

| Section | What goes in it |
|---|---|
| **Heading** | The product name, in terms the customer understands. |
| **Subheading** | One sentence: who the product is for and the benefit they get. |
| **Summary** | A short paragraph — city & (future) date, the product, and the core benefit. Assume the reader stops here; it must stand alone. |
| **Problem** | The customer's pain, written from *their* point of view. The sharper and more real the problem, the stronger the PR. |
| **Solution** | How the product solves the problem — simply. What the experience is, not how it's built. |
| **Quotes & call to action** | A quote from a company spokesperson, a quote from a (hypothetical) delighted customer, and how to get started. |

**Test:** if the press release isn't compelling to a customer, the idea isn't
ready — fix the idea, not the prose.

## Part 2 — FAQ (≤ 5 pages)

The honest interrogation. Two kinds of questions:

- **External (customer / press):** what a customer or journalist would ask — how
  it works, what it costs, how it compares, why now.
- **Internal (the hard ones — this is where ideas die cheaply):**
  - **Market size / TAM** — how big is this, really?
  - **Per-unit economics & P&L** — does a single unit/customer make money? What's the cost structure?
  - **Dependencies & third-party risk** — what (or whom) does this rely on that you don't control?
  - **Feasibility** — what's technically hard or unproven? What has to be true?
  - **Why us / why now** — what's the unfair advantage and the timing?

Put the questions you're *afraid* of in the FAQ. Skipping them makes the document
dishonest and defers the pain to a more expensive moment. Mock-ups, wireframes,
and supporting data go in an appendix (not required in-session reading).

## Running the review

- Distribute the PR-FAQ **at** the meeting; open with a **silent read** (no presenter walk-through).
- General reactions first, then **line-by-line**.
- **Seniors speak last** to avoid anchoring the room.
- Revise from the criticism. Repeat with successively more senior groups.
- Decide: **build / kill / iterate.** Most don't get built — that's the system working.
